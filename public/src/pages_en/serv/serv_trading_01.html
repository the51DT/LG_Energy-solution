Overview

LG Energy Solution’s power trading business connects renewable power plants with the electricity market through an ESS and ICT-based Virtual Power Plant (VPP) platform. As a leading participant in Korea’s renewable energy bidding pilot project, the company has pioneered multiple industry-first initiatives including the nation’s first stand-alone ESS power plant linked to the distribution grid and demonstration projects that alleviate nighttime grid overloads using ESS. Through these efforts, LG Energy Solution is advancing grid stability, enhancing market efficiency, and contributing to the sustainable growth of the energy ecosystem.

EaaS

Energy-as-a-Service (EaaS) represents a new paradigm that goes beyond traditional energy production and consumption by delivering integrated solutions encompassing power generation, storage, trading, and optimization. Through this service, customers can entrust the entire energy management process to the platform - free from operational complexity while achieving greater efficiency and profitability.
By leveraging AI-driven technologies and its extensive experience in operating ESS and renewable energy systems, LG Energy Solution continues to expand innovative and sustainable energy services across global markets, redefining how energy is managed and utilized.

Service Offerings

VPP Operation

Integrating distributed energy resources into a single virtual power plant, providing functions such as generation forecasting, real-time power trading, and grid stabilization.

AI Based bid&dispatch optimization

Delivering end-to-end support from developing customized bidding strategies to executing power market transactions on behalf of customers.

Renewable Energy Forecasting

Utilizing weather data, facility information, historical operation records, and AI models to deliver highly accurate generation forecasts.

Distribution Load Relief (NWAs)

By utilizing stand-alone ESS connected to the distribution grid, congestion can be bypassed and efficient power distribution enabled, thereby improving transmission and distribution efficiency while contributing to grid stability.
