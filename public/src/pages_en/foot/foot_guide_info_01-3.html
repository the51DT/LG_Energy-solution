Lithium-ion Battery Safety Guide For End-users

1. Overview

LG Energy Solution prioritizes user safety and provides the following safety guidelines to prevent accidents caused by the misuse of lithium-ion batteries.

1. Purpose and Limitations
  • This end-user safety guide is intended solely as a reference for general information about LG Energy Solution’s lithium-ion battery products.
2. Limitation of Liability
  • LG Energy Solution is not responsible for any damage, loss, injury, or property damage arising from the following situations:
    1. Use of products obtained through unauthorized or illegal distribution channels.
    2. Use of products that have been disassembled, reassembled, modified, or altered without LG Energy Solution’s approval.
    3. Use in a manner that conflicts with the instructions and safety guidelines provided by the final product manufacturer.
    4. Use of the information in this guide for unintended purposes or in unintended ways.
  • Lithium-ion batteries can pose serious risks, including fire, explosion, severe injury, or death if misused. Always purchase the final products through official channels and use them according to the final product manufacturer’s instructions.

LG Energy Solution provides no express or implied warranties, including but not limited to merchantability or fitness for a particular purpose, regarding the information in this guide.

2. Battery Cell and Module Safety Warnings

All lithium-ion battery cells and modules sold by LG Energy Solution are industrial components that must be used in the battery pack with
protective circuitry*. They are not designed or manufactured for sale or use by individual consumers (or end-users).
LG Energy Solution does not sell battery cells or modules to individual consumers (or end-users) through any retail, e-commerce, or auction channels.

Protective circuitry : A component part mounted externally on lithium-ion battery cells or modules that protects against abnormal operation such as overcharging,
over-discharging, overcurrent, and overheating. This includes Battery Management Systems (BMS).

Lithium-ion battery cells and modules store a large amount of energy internally, and misuse can lead to fire or explosion, causing serious personal injury or property damage.
    1. Handling lithium-ion battery cells or modules as an individual consumer (or end-users) may result in fire or explosion accidents.
      LG Energy Solution is not responsible for any physical, property, or personal injury resulting from such incidents.
    2. Do not use LG Energy Solution-manufactured lithium-ion battery cells or modules in devices intended for individual consumer (or end-user) use, such as e-cigarettes or portable lanterns, where they are meant to be replaced, removed, or recharged by the user.
    3. Do not remove lithium-ion battery cells or modules from a battery pack, as this may damage the protective circuitry or the cells.
    4. LG Energy Solution does not distribute or sell lithium-ion batteries through retail, e-commerce, or auction channels.
      Do not purchase LG Energy Solution lithium-ion battery cells or modules from these sources for sale or use.

5. Precautions for Product Disposal

Under the Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) system, battery cells, modules, packs, and electronic products containing batteries must be properly collected and recycled by the product manufacturers.

If an individual consumer (or end-user) unintentionally acquires a lithium-ion battery cell, module, or pack that lacks a protection circuit, is damaged, or has expired its warranty, please do not charge, use, or repurpose it for other devices. Instead, follow the guidelines below and return the product to the manufacturer:
    1. Seal the (+) and (–) terminals or connection points of the lithium-ion battery cell, module, or pack with tape to prevent contact with other batteries or metal objects.
    2. Do not stack or store lithium-ion battery cells, modules, or packs with other items in bags or boxes, or store them with other batteries. Avoid placing metal objects near them. Do not store lithium-ion battery cells with coins, keys, or other metal objects in pockets or bags.
    3. Do not dispose of lithium-ion batteries as general waste. Do not dispose of lithium-ion batteries in fire.
    4. For further details, contact your local waste management authority or the retailer where you purchased the product.
If you wish to dispose of a final product containing a battery, follow these guidelines and return it to the manufacturer:
    1. Do not attempt to remove the battery from the product. Return the final product with the battery installed to its manufacturer. Exception: For replaceable battery models, follow the manufacturer’s instructions for battery removal.
    2. Do not dispose of final products with batteries as general waste. Do not dispose of final products with batteries in fire.
    3. For further details, contact your local waste management authority or the retailer where you purchased the product.
