Waste Battery Transport and Disposal Guidelines

1. Transport and Disposal Precautions

  • Always comply with local and national waste management regulations when transporting or disposing of batteries.
  • Do not discard lithium-ion batteries in household waste or general trash bins.

    - For disposal or recycling procedures, contact your local waste management authority or the place of purchase.

  • Seal the battery terminals (+) and (–) with insulating tape or terminal caps to prevent short circuits.
  • Do not transport or dispose of LG Energy Solution lithium-ion batteries together with other types of batteries.
  • Damaged batteries must be stabilized (e.g., via saline treatment) and securely packaged in accordance with DDR packaging requirements.
    Also, appropriate hazardous material labels must be attached.
  • When transporting batteries, ensure compliance with ADR (European Agreement concerning the International Carriage of Dangerous Goods by Road)
    and other relevant regulations.
  • Do not store lithium-ion batteries with other items in uncertified containers such as bags, drawers, or boxes.

    - Used batteries may retain residual charge and storing them with other items may lead to overheating and fire.

  • In the event of a fire during transport, it must be promptly reported to the fire department for appropriate response.
  • For safety assurance and improved recycling efficiency, please discharge the battery using an electrical discharge method prior to disposal.

2. Precautions for Disposal of ESS, Portable, and Automotive Batteries

  • Do not use or store the following types of batteries under any circumstances. Dispose of them immediately in accordance with local regulations:
    1. Batteries that have experienced abnormal reactions such as electrical shock, drops, impact, fire (including high heat), or moisture ingress.
    2. Batteries with visible external damage such as deformation, breakage, rupture, swelling, or corrosion.
    3. Batteries exhibiting abnormal performance or suspected malfunction.
  • In accordance with the EU Battery Regulation, these batteries are designed to enable reuse, disassembly, and material recovery where technically
    and economically feasible.
  • During the disposal process, separate collection and appropriate treatment methods are recommended to enable resource circulation.
  • LG Energy Solution prohibits unauthorized reuse, resale, or non-approved disposal methods, and for any losses or damages resulting from such actions.
