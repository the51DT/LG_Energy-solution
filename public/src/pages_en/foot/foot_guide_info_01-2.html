Energy Storage System(ESS) Product Safety Guide

ESS batteries contain electrical, physical, chemical, and environmental risks. Strict adherence to these safety guidelines is required.

This safety guide applies to both Grid ESS and Residential ESS. Some sections are tailored to the specific characteristics of each system.
Please refer to the full document for details.

The content of this safety guide may be periodically updated or revised as the scope of product environmental regulations expands.

1. Electrical Hazards

  • LG Energy Solution ESS products are safe under normal conditions. However, damaged batteries may pose high-voltage and electric shock risks.
    Exercise caution if the enclosure, rack, pack, or safety circuit is damaged.
  • Do not immerse the battery pack in liquid, puncture, break, short-circuit, incinerate, or forcibly discharge it.
    These actions can result in Internal or external short circuits, leading to severe overheating.

2. Mechanical Damage Risks

  • Store battery packs in their original packaging to prevent mechanical damage.
  • Dropping battery products can cause severe damage. Conduct a visual inspection after a drop, and discontinue use if damage is suspected.
    Damaged batteries should be stored separately and transported in an insulated condition.

3. High-Temperature Exposure Risk

  • ESS batteries operate normally at 0–40°C and <85% non-condensing humidity.
  • At 80°C or higher, electrolyte leakage may occur.
  • Exposure to temperatures above 150°C for several minutes can cause thermal runaway and fire risk.

4. Electrolyte Leakage Risk

  • Mechanical damage may cause electrolyte leakage from the cell. Leaked electrolyte contains volatile organic compounds and fluorine compounds,
    which can irritate skin and respiratory system.
  • In the event of a leak, immediately ventilate the area and handle it while wearing appropriate personal protective equipment.

5. Emission Gas Risk

  • ESS batteries do not emit gases during normal operation. However, severe thermal runaway or abnormal heating may cause gas emissions,
    which can be flammable and toxic. Avoid exposure and immediately notify local fire services and emergency responders.

6. Fire Response

  • If a high-temperature event occurs in the battery enclosure, do not open the enclosure door, as it may explode. Stay at least 10 meters away from the enclosure.
    Immediately report any smoke or fire to the nearest fire department and LG Energy Solution representative.
  • In the event of fire department dispatch, whether to proceed with additional water application to the exterior of the enclosure must be executed in consultation with
    LG Energy Solution representatives.
  • Do not cut off internal power to ensure the Active Ventilation System (AVS) operates properly (except when AVS is powered by an UPS).
  • Water is the most effective extinguishing agent for battery fires. Use large amounts of water to cool the cells and suppress thermal runaway propagation.

7. Handling and Storage

  • Do not expose batteries to extreme temperatures or fire, disassemble or compress them, puncture them, overcharge or over-discharge them,
    short-circuit the positive and negative terminals, or place them on conductive metal surfaces.
  • Always wear insulated gloves and protective equipment when handling ESS batteries.
  • Store batteries away from direct sunlight, high temperatures, and moisture.
  • Charging without approval from LG Energy Solution is prohibited. Only charging and discharging through compatible inverters are allowed.
    Even if forced charging is necessary, it is prohibited for anyone other than LG Energy Solution / ASC personnel to perform forced charging.
